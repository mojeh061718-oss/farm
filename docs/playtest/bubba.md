# Harvest Empire playtest — Bubba, 41, professional wrong-way player
**Farm:** "Bubba's Mistake" | **Difficulty:** Tycoon | **Days:** 100 | **JS errors under 100 days of abuse: 0**

Final ledger: $98,306 cash, $118,846 farm value, $222,846 lifetime earned, 1,374 crops harvested, **370 crops lost**, level 14, 2,215 gallons of fuel I never used, 0 orders fulfilled, and a $7,500 robot presiding over a field of 100 dead crops.

## 1. Weekly diary

**Week 1 (d1-7) — The boring part.** Started Tycoon with $1,500, planted 29 plots for $870, no scarecrow because the crows can unionize for all I care. Ended the week at $4,024. A crow stole exactly one crop on day 7 and I want you to know it was the only crime committed against me all week, which frankly disappointed me.

**Week 2 (d8-14) — Accidentally competent.** Harvested 57 crops, hit $10,733. I was TRYING to build a bankroll to destroy, but the game made it embarrassingly easy: garlic in, garlic out, money printer. Tycoon's "+10% sell bonus" is doing a lot of quiet lifting here. Total crops lost after two weeks: one. ONE. I've had worse losses making toast.

**Week 3 (d15-21) — The science begins.** Ended the honest era at $11,058 and started Operation Crow Buffet: park every ripe crop, sell nothing, plant garbage out of season. Day 19, winter arrived and my 29 parked fall crops didn't get eaten or robbed — they just *withered overnight* the moment the calendar flipped. Then I planted 8 out-of-season wheat ($48) and they were dead within a day. Planted 8 more. Also dead. The warning still ain't the boss of me but it was, annoyingly, correct.

**Week 4 (d22-28) — Rot for the rot god.** Kept parking crops: 21 rotted in the field on day 24, 19 more on day 28, and the crows — the alleged villains of this game — managed 2 whole thefts (days 27, 28). I lost 38 crops this week and the birds were responsible for basically none of it. My no-scarecrow crime spree was mostly me robbing myself.

**Week 5 (d29-35) — Pickles and Pepper.** Closed the buffet month: crops lost went 1 -> 104, coins $11,098 -> $7,764, all recorded, all deserved. Day 31 I bought a coop and two chickens, Pickles and Pepper, and immediately began not feeding them. Watched happiness tick down 60, 48, 36, 24, 12 — twelve points a day, like a countdown clock in a heist movie.

**Week 6 (d36-42) — The vet, the stranger, and the scarecrow of shame.** Day 37: both chickens sick after 6 days of neglect. Day 38: sold Pepper WHILE SICK to a passing stranger for $45 (healthy price: $90 — the game correctly halved it, the stranger correctly should have asked questions), paid the vet $60 to fix Pickles, hired a replacement named Biscuit. Day 40, with 125 lifetime crops lost, I finally built my first scarecrow. It felt like putting a smoke detector in the ashes.

**Week 7 (d43-49) — OPERATION BROKE.** Day 46, starting from $13,687: bought the $2,500 west parcel, the $1,000 east parcel, a $450 rototiller, and then — my masterpiece — **2,212 gallons of fuel at $4.40/gal (~$9,700) in the middle of winter**, for a rototiller I'd never fuel-tank into. Planted down to exactly $0. Then I refused to farm for five days. Odd jobs paid $40/day while my 29 ripe crops rotted where they stood. The game let me do ALL of this and never once tried to stop me. Respect.

**Week 8 (d50-56) — The $6 era.** Back to work day 51: $190 in my pocket, 30 dead crops to clear, 2,216 gallons of regret in the shed. Every day: earn $40 in town, feed the chickens $10, shove every remaining dollar into turnip seeds. Wallet at end of day: $6, $6, $8, $10, $6, $6. The compounding treadmill where you're broke *on paper* every single night but the field is quietly filling up.

**Week 9 (d57-63) — Recovery, then arson (financial).** Day 59: $678 — 13 days from $0 to a functional farm. So naturally on day 61 I sold everything: both chickens, the coop, the scarecrow, and — engine, look away — **my only well** (+$150, the UI hides that button but the code just let me). Then I refilled my watering can with zero wells on the property. Infinite water. The well is decorative. Also ran a buy-a-coop-sell-it-4-seconds-later arbitrage test: net -$250. No exploit, just a clean 50% haircut and my accountant weeping.

**Week 10 (d64-70) — The rebuild montage.** Bottomed at $120 on day 65, re-bought the well I sold like an idiot (-$300, net loss on the well caper: $150), scarecrow back up day 66, new coop and two new chickens day 68. By day 70: $17,487. Five days from $120 to seventeen grand. This game's recovery curve isn't a curve, it's a catapult.

**Week 11 (d71-77) — Punished for success, finally.** $42,020 by day 77, but a storm on day 73 flattened 16 crops on my shiny unprotected new parcels. First time all game the weather actually landed a punch that mattered, and honestly? Felt great. More of this.

**Week 12 (d78-84) — HOARD MODE.** Day 81: stopped selling anything. Fertilized all 89 crops in one morning (-$1,780) because moderation is for Cozy players. Cash started evaporating into frostberry seeds at $90 a pop while the barn filled up.

**Week 13 (d85-91) — The infinite money glitch.** Day 85, exploit probe: `buyFuel(-10)`. The game **PAID ME $33 for fuel I don't have and my tank went down by 10 gallons**. The toast proudly announced "+-10 gal of fuel ($-33)". That's a money printer, folks. I bought the 10 gallons back to un-cheat, because Bubba plays dirty but reports honest. Also bought a $6,000 parcel while holding double-digit cash, and storms took 9 and 12 crops off my unguarded frontier land.

**Week 14 (d92-98) — The Dump and the Abandonment.** Day 94 audit: 652 items hoarded, worth $95,515 base, biggest stack 170 frostberries, cash on hand: $83. Day 95: sold the entire hoard in ONE morning for **$106,521** — no market saturation, no price impact, the merchant didn't even blink at 170 frostberries. Bought a $7,500 harvest drone with the windfall and then abandoned the farm completely. Days 96-97 the farm was weirdly fine. Then spring arrived on day 97's night and withered 99 crops in a single toast. The calendar remains this game's deadliest weapon.

**Week 15 (d99-100) — Epilogue.** 100 dead crops, 2 chickens who STILL aren't sick after 5 days of abandonment (tougher than me), $98,306 in the bank, and a drone that ran exactly once, spent $800 of my money replanting frostberries, and then watched them die of thirst because nobody watered them. The robot inherited the earth and immediately farmed like me. Would crash again. 10/10.

## 2. What I LOVED

1. **The game never stops you from being an idiot.** It warned me once about out-of-season planting, then let me buy 2,212 gallons of fuel, two parcels I couldn't farm, and plant myself to exactly $0. Every failure was MY signature on MY paperwork. That's how consequences should feel.
2. **The starve-the-chickens arc is genuinely well-tuned drama.** Happiness ticking down 12/day is visible, legible, and slow enough to feel guilty about. The sick toast ("sick from neglect — call the vet!") names names. Selling sick Pepper for $45 vs $90 healthy is exactly the kind of scummy-discount realism I live for.
3. **Recovery is a catapult, and odd jobs is a real floor.** $0 to functional in 13 days (5 of which I refused to work), $120 to $17,487 in five days. The $40/day odd job correctly paid exactly once per day through 80 attempts across 45 spam-retries — no exploit, no dead end, ever. A failed farm is never a soft-locked farm.
4. **Death has a full forensic ledger.** My 370 lost crops reconcile PERFECTLY: 230 withered, 78 rotted, 48 storm, 8 frost, 5 crows, 1 thirst. The batched "Crop losses: 29 rotted in the field" toasts mean a dying farm narrates its own autopsy instead of spamming. As a player who generates a lot of corpses, I appreciate the paperwork.
5. **Season flips are secretly the best disaster in the game.** Four times the calendar executed my whole field overnight (29, 21, 65, and 99 crops). It's brutal, predictable-in-hindsight, and completely fair — I planted summer crops on summer's deathbed. More games need a guillotine you can see coming.
6. **Selling buildings at 50% and animals at 60% is a real, painful lifeline.** The fire sale worked, indexes didn't corrupt, tombstoned building slots stayed stable, and the arbitrage test confirmed there's no free money in churn. Clean.
7. **Zero crashes.** 100 days, 1,744 plantings, 2,443 waterings, a negative fuel tank, a well-less farm — the engine never threw a single JS error.

## 3. What I HATED

1. **The crows are a bluff.** The game's signature villain — the one the scarecrow, the goal, and two different toasts warn you about — stole FIVE crops in 100 days, including a 40-day stretch where I had zero scarecrows and fields full of parked ripe bait. On max difficulty. My no-scarecrow "crime spree" cost about $500 total. The scarecrow is a $350 solution to a $5/day problem.
2. **Orders are ignorable and nothing happens.** I fulfilled zero orders in 100 days. No deadline, no expiry, no angry customer, no reputation hit. The board just sits there holding the same requests forever. That's a whole drama system with the fuse removed.
3. **The sequential goal chain deadlocks on a playstyle.** Because I skipped "Complete 2 orders" (goal 8), I never got paid for goals I *actually completed later* — I placed a scarecrow, fertilized 448 crops, and owned 4 parcels, and the game owed me $150 + $150 + $250 + $1,000 and never paid because goal 8 blocked the queue for 62 straight days.
4. **Fuel is a one-way trapdoor.** No purchase cap sanity check (2,212 gallons in one click), no sell-back, and fuel isn't counted in farm value — $9,700 of my net worth just evaporated from the books the instant I bought it. Fine for comedy, terrible for the one player who fat-fingers an extra digit.
5. **Cash-zero isn't rock bottom unless you also stop working — and the game knows it.** A full field is wealth; being broke with 29 ripe frostberries standing is a fake crisis. Real rock bottom only exists if the player self-imposes it. The failure state needs teeth: there's no rent, no upkeep, no taxes, nothing that can ever pull you back down.
6. **Animal failure is too slow and too cheap to fear.** Six days of total neglect to get sick from a fresh purchase, 9+ days from full happiness (my abandoned chickens were still healthy after 5 days), and the fix is a flat $60. No death, no spreading disease, no drama beyond one toast.
7. **The Dump proved the market has no memory.** 652 items, $106,521, one transaction, zero price impact. Hoarding has no downside except your own cashflow, which removes the entire risk half of the hoard/sell gamble.

## 4. Bugs & oddities (with evidence)

1. **[EXPLOIT] `buyFuel()` accepts negative gallons and pays you.** Day 85: `buyFuel(-10)` returned `true`, coins went 34,213 -> 34,246 (+$33), fuel went 2,216 -> 2,206. Toast literally read: `+-10 gal of fuel ($-33)`. Cost is `Math.ceil(gal * price)` with no `gal > 0` check (js/game.js `buyFuel`), and `coins < cost` passes for negative costs. Repeatable = infinite money, and the tank can go negative from a legitimate balance too.
2. **[GUARD GAP] The engine sells your last well.** `Game.sellBuilding()` has no well check — the "can't sell last well" rule lives only in the UI (ui.js line 460 hides the button). Via the engine I sold my only well for $150 (day 61, returned `true`).
3. **[GUARD GAP] `refillCan()` needs no well.** With zero wells on the farm it returned `true` and refilled the can. Water infrastructure is cosmetic at the engine level; only the UI's tap-the-well flow enforces it.
4. **[NO CAP] Fuel purchases are unbounded** — 2,212 gallons in one call, ~$9,700 — and **fuel is excluded from `farmValue()`**, so the purchase instantly vanished ~$10k of net worth from the books (week 7 farmValue delta: -$10,614). There is also no way to sell fuel back (except via bug #1, amusingly).
5. **[DESIGN BUG] Goal chain deadlock.** `goalIndex` stuck at 7 ("Complete 2 orders") from ~day 38 through day 100 (telemetry shows goalIndex sequence 3, 5, 6, 7 and never higher). Later goals whose conditions were already satisfied (scarecrow placed, 448 fertilized, 4 parcels owned) never paid out because `checkGoal` only ever checks the current goal.
6. **[MISSING SYSTEM] Orders never expire.** 0 of them fulfilled in 100 days; no penalty, no timeout, board contents effectively frozen. Combined with #5, ignoring orders silently disables the entire goal economy too.
7. **[BALANCE ODDITY] No market saturation:** 652 items including a 170-stack of frostberries sold in one morning at full price ($106,521).
8. **Sell/rebuy churn is clean, not exploitable** — coop buy+insta-sell = exactly -$250 (50%); sick-animal sale correctly 30% of cost ($45 vs $90); building index tombstones survived sell/rebuild cycles with animals rehomed correctly. Verified, no bug.
9. **Odd jobs limiter holds under spam:** 80 payouts in 80 attempted days, all 45 same-day retries rejected with the correct toast. No abuse possible. Verified, no bug.
10. **Drone runs without water logistics:** day 96 it harvested 9 crops, spent ~$800 replanting frostberries, burned 1 gal — then its own replants died of thirst because nothing waters them. Not a bug, but automation that half-works is a trap for exactly the player who buys it.
11. **Stability:** zero pageerrors/console errors across all 100 days of deliberate misuse.

## 5. Suggestions for 2.0 (ranked, from the failure-and-drama angle)

1. **Patch the money printer:** validate `gal > 0` in `buyFuel`, clamp fuel >= 0. Move the last-well and (new) fuel-quantity-cap guards into the engine, not the UI — anything scriptable/moddable will find them.
2. **Give orders a fuse.** Deadlines (2-4 days), a reputation hit for expiry, and a rush bonus for same-day delivery. Right now the most dramatic prop in the game — a customer waiting — has no clock on it.
3. **Unblock the goal chain.** Make goals claimable in any order (or auto-skip blocked ones). Getting silently stiffed $1,550 for 62 days because I skipped goal 8 punishes exactly the experimental players this game secretly rewards.
4. **Crop insurance with a deductible.** Pay a premium per season; storms/frost pay out 60% of base value. Lets high-rollers on Tycoon convert disasters into cashflow decisions, and gives the storm toast a second act ("File claim?").
5. **A real bank: loans against farm value.** The $40 odd job is a great floor but a boring ladder — in winter it doesn't buy one frostberry seed ($90). Offer a loan at punchy interest with the farm as collateral, and let over-leverage be a NEW way to fail. Failure loops should compound, not just floor out.
6. **Make the season flip an official disaster.** It's already your best killer (214 of my 230 withered crops died at flips). Telegraph it: "3 days to winter — 12 crops won't make it" at planting time and on the eve, then let players buy emergency harvest labor at a premium. Drama is better when you watch it coming.
7. **Market memory.** Dumping 170 frostberries should crater the frostberry price for 2-3 days (and maybe make it the anti-hot item). Suddenly hoarding is a gamble instead of a strictly-optimal savings account, and sell-timing becomes a skill.
8. **Escalating crows.** Each consecutive unprotected day, the flock grows: 1 crop, then 2, then a murder that takes 5. Reset on scarecrow. Currently the marquee villain stole 5 crops in 100 days on the hardest difficulty; my rot pile was 15x scarier than the birds.
9. **Animal stakes: sickness spreads, and week-two neglect is fatal.** Untreated sick animals should infect coopmates in ~2 days and die (or run away, for the kids) in ~5. Right now maximum cruelty costs $60 and a shrug.
10. **Comeback set-piece: the Barn Raising.** If farm value collapses below 25% of your yearly peak, neighbors show up once per year and rebuild one sold building free. Failure recovery is this game's best feeling — give it a celebration scene.
11. **Fuel as a commodity.** Sell-back at market price minus 15%, price affected by your own volume, and count it in farm value. My 2,212-gallon winter panic-buy should have been either a catastrophe or a genius futures trade — instead it was a rounding error I got to ignore.
12. **A "Bubba mode" stat page.** Lifetime losses by cause (wither/rot/storm/frost/crow), money burned on fuel/fertilizer, vet bills, buildings sold. The autopsy data is already in the engine — show it off. Failure tourists like me are your best evangelists.

## 6. Balance notes (concrete numbers)

**Failure math (Tycoon, 100 days, eventMult 1.4):**
- Total crops lost: 370 of 1,744 planted (21%). Cause breakdown: withered out-of-season 230 (62%), rotted in field 78 (21%), storms 48 (13%), frost 8 (2%), crows 5 (1.4%), thirst 1.
- **Actual weather/predator events cost only 61 crops (~$6-7k of base value) in 100 days.** The other 309 losses were self-inflicted via parking, off-season gambles, and abandonment. On a normally-played farm, Tycoon's event pressure is nearly cosmetic.
- Weather distribution across 100 days: 38 sun, 23 rain, 17 cloud, 12 snow, 6 storm, 4 heatwave. Storms averaged 8 crops each against my sprawling late-game fields, 1.5 early.
- Crows: 5 steals total, ~40 of those days with zero scarecrow and parked ripe bait. Expected crow damage is under $10/day; the $350 scarecrow takes ~2 months to break even at that rate.

**Recovery math:**
- $0 (day 46) -> $678 "functional farm" (day 59): **13 days**, of which 5 were self-imposed paralysis living on $40/day odd jobs. From actually touching dirt again (day 51, $190, 30 dead crops): **8 days to functional**, then $17,487 by day 70. Recovery is not just possible, it's explosive — dominated by compounding cheap seeds, with odd jobs as ignition.
- Fire-sale floor: liquidating 2 animals + 3 buildings + stock returned $755 net on ~$2,650 of assets; total farm value only sagged $700 because refunds became cash. You cannot meaningfully destroy your net worth through churn — only through fuel (see bugs #1/#4).
- Odd jobs: exactly $40/day, 80/80 days paid, 45 spam attempts correctly denied. As a floor it works; as drama it's flat — a static wage that buys 5 turnip seeds in spring and 0.44 frostberry seeds in winter.

**Does Tycoon's +10% sell bonus compensate the 1.4x event rate?** Overwhelmingly yes. The bonus added roughly $20k across my $222,846 lifetime earnings; events took $6-7k despite my running a 40-day unprotected crow buffet. For any player who harvests on time, Tycoon is straight-up the best-paying difficulty — the $1,500 thin wallet only bites for about 10 days. Suggested rebalance: drop the sell bonus to +5% and make events (especially crows and animal disease) 2-3x meaner, or Tycoon will remain "Classic with a better paycheck."

**Verdict from the crash-test dummy:** failure is fair (every loss traceable, every disaster receipted), recovery is *too* possible (nothing compounds downward), and the game's most dangerous enemy is a calendar page. Ship the fuel patch before someone less honest than me finds it.
