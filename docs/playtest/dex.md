# Harvest Empire — 100-Day Tycoon Playtest Review
**Player persona: Dex, 27. Min-maxer. Difficulty: Tycoon ($1,500 start, +10% sell, 1.4x hazard rate).**
**Best run: "Margin Call Farms" — final farm value $877,481, lifetime earned $1,095,101, level 20, all 18 goals, 6,095 harvests, 27 crops lost (0.4%), zero sick animals, zero JS errors in 200 simulated days across 2 runs.**

I read the source before planting a single seed (you would too — the game has no wiki, and the numbers ARE the game). Core discovery that drives everything: a day is 48s, and with once-a-day check-ins your effective crop cycle is grow-time rounded UP to whole days. Fertilizer ($20, +25% speed, +45% double-harvest) compresses cabbage from a 3-dawn to a 2-dawn cycle. That one rounding interaction is worth more than every building in the game.

Day 12 was goal-cascade day: collect-5, 2-orders, scarecrow, 2nd-parcel and fertilize-5 all popped in one session, +$830. **Week 4 — FV $61,524 (+$22.1k).**

## 2. What I LOVED

1. **Fertilizer is a real decision with real math.** $20 for +25% speed and +45% double-harvest is a trap on a turnip (+$7 EV) and a monster on cabbage (+$74 EV *and* it compresses the cycle from 3 dawns to 2 because of day-rounding). Items that are good or bad *depending on context* are exactly what a min-max game needs. Best-designed number in the game.
2. **No level gates anywhere.** Buildings, land, crops — if you have cash, you can buy it. My entire route was cash-limited, never arbitrarily time-gated. Speedrunner heaven; Hay Day could never.
3. **Goals reward, never gate — and cascade retroactively.** Day 37: buying the drone completed goal 15 and instantly popped two already-satisfied goals for +$3,500 total. Backlogged achievement payouts respect the player's ability to sequence-break. 18/18 completed and I never once had to do something purely "for the quest."
4. **Regrow crops are an engine-builder's dream.** Strawberry/tomato/grapes convert a one-time seed into a permanent daily dividend. Day-1 all-in on strawberries is the correct open and the game lets you find it.
5. **Tomorrow's forecast is visible.** `s.forecast` is real, actionable information (rain tomorrow = skip watering; storm = check coverage). More sims should let you play the weather instead of suffering it.
6. **Hazards are solvable, not random taxes.** 12 scarecrow positions can blanket the entire map for ~$4,200. I only placed 8 (my own placement bug) and still cut losses to 27 crops in 100 days. Deterministic protection auras with clean 5x5 geometry — good.
7. **The safety-net stack.** Odd jobs ($40/day income floor), 50% building sell-back, 60% animal sell-back, 3 rotating save backups, checksummed export codes. You cannot brick this save. Respect.
8. **Dawn ordering is correct:** sprinklers water, THEN drones harvest and replant. Someone thought about the pipeline order. As a factory-game player, I noticed and I approve.

## 3. What I HATED

1. **Watering is 6,181 taps of nothing.** It's not a decision — every unwatered crop is strictly worse, so it's a chore with extra steps. My log shows 1,030 can refills. The endgame answer (sprinklers) waters a measly 3x3 and would need ~25 units eating 25 crop tiles to cover my farm. Melvor would have automated this by hour two.
2. **13 of 18 crops are noob traps.** Wheat: $9.4/tile/day. Fert-cabbage: $64.3. That's a 7x spread between two things sitting next to each other in the same shop with the same click cost. Potato, rice, carrot, turnip, kale, sunflower, pepper — never planted after week 1, and pepper's 50s regrow (2s over the day boundary!) makes it strictly worse than tomato's 45s.
3. **Animals (except sheep) are decorative.** Cow: $850 for ~$16.6/day net = 53-day payback while any dirt tile pays back in 2. Pig: $3,200 for ~$51/day = 63 days, and truffles feed no recipe. I bought cows for the goal and the cake line; the creamery they justify sat idle basically all game because 4 cows make 1.7 milk/day. Collected 350 animal products in 100 days — my cabbage did 6,095.
4. **Crafting is capped by a 3-slot queue and >48s job times.** Every recipe (50-150s) outlasts the 48s day, so a daily-check player gets 1.5 crafts/day per building, period. My press's grape juice (+$257 value-add/craft) is mathematically great and physically starving. The creamery is the worst offender: idle 90% of the run.
5. **Grow times are pre-rounded against you.** 110s = 2.29 days, 50s = 1.04 days, 125s = 2.6 days — nearly every crop overshoots a day boundary by a few percent, so you eat a full extra day at any sane check-in rhythm. And day 1 starting at t=0.25 made my strawberries miss harvest by literally 1 second (84s grown, 85s needed), which delayed harvest a day and fed a berry to a crow.
6. **Tilling is irreversible and living crops can't be removed.** No shovel, no way to restore grass. I had to plan every future building's footprint on day 1, and when the greenhouse made my tomato field obsolete I had to *deliberately drought-execute* healthy tomatoes over 2 days to convert to cabbage. Executing your own crops via neglect being the optimal (and only) conversion tool is grim.
7. **Orders are a rounding error.** Flat 1.6x BASE — ignores the +10% Tycoon bonus, ignores rep (+19% for me), ignores market multipliers. Selling on a hot day pays up to ~3.1x base. Orders produced $18,235 of my $1,095,101 lifetime (1.7%), and half of that was one $8,910 ticket. My main interaction with the order board was the skip button.
8. **The economy has no fourth act.** All meaningful purchases were done day 53. I finished with $437k cash + $178k inventory and zero sinks. Weeks 11-15 were pure autopilot; farm value growth even *decelerated* relative to assets because income is linear and there is nothing to compound into.
9. **Toast spam.** 1,515 toasts in 100 days, over 1,000 of them "Watering can refilled!". Every dawn is a notification blizzard for routine actions.

## 4. Bugs & Exploits

**THE DOMINANT STRATEGY (verified over the full run):**
Day 1 all-in strawberries → tomatoes in summer → **fertilized cabbage monoculture forever**, all cash into land in ascending cost order, scarecrow grid, greenhouse only for regrow/artisan-input continuity, sheep+looms as the only animals, warehouse goods and dump on price spikes. $/tile/day at Tycoon (avg market mult, incl. double-harvest EV):

| Crop (mode) | Cycle @ daily check-in | Net $/tile/day |
|---|---|---|
| **Cabbage + fert** | 2 dawns | **$64.3** |
| Frostberry + fert (winter) | 3 dawns | $67.5 |
| Pumpkin + fert | 3 dawns | $66.0 |
| Melon + fert | 3 dawns | $57.7 |
| Tomato (regrow) | 1 dawn | $52.3 |
| Grapes (regrow) | 2 dawns | $52.2 |
| Strawberry (regrow) | 1 dawn | $42.8 |
| Corn + fert | 2 dawns | $45.6 |
| Kale + fert | 2 dawns | $44.8 |
| Turnip | 1 dawn | $12.2 |
| Wheat | 1 dawn | $9.4 |
| Chicken (per animal) | — | ~$12.6 |
| Cow (per animal) | — | ~$16.6 |
| Pig (per animal) | — | ~$51 |
| **Sheep → quilt (per animal)** | — | **~$113-137** |
| Cow + creamery cheese | — | ~$40 |
| Cake line (full footprint incl. coop/barn/wheat/berry tiles) | — | ~$52-64/tile — ties dirt |

The punchline: **the fanciest value chain in the game (wheat+egg+milk+strawberry → cake) nets the same per tile as fertilized cabbage**, with 100x the capital and complexity. Only sheep→quilt clearly beats dirt.

**Exploits / broken things, with evidence:**
1. **Greenhouse is a global flag, not a building.** `seasonOK()` returns true for *every tile on the map* if any greenhouse exists. One $12k purchase permanently deletes the season system from a 300-tile map. It doesn't even need crops near it.
2. **Greenhouse is also nearly redundant** — the frost event only kills non-winter crops, so a winter-crop rotation (frostberry-fert at $67.5/day, the game's best number) never fears frost. Its flagship feature protects against a hazard optimal play already avoids.
3. **XP per-call rounding:** `sellItem` grants `ceil(qty/2)` XP **per call**. Selling 80 items one at a time yields 80 XP vs 40 in bulk — doubled rep XP for free. I did this all run (level 20, +19% prices). Evidence: `stats.sold = 6,689` across thousands of unit sales.
4. **`refillCan()` has no location check in the API** — the well is pure UI theater. (Cheap shot from a bot, but the sim/UI contract mismatch is real.)
5. **Crows are structurally irrelevant** to anyone harvesting at dawn: they only eat *ripe* crops and only at midday; a dawn-harvested farm has nothing ripe at noon. One berry lost in 100 days — and only because of the day-1 t=0.25 rounding bug below.
6. **Day 1 starts at t=0.25**, silently shorting every first-day crop 12 seconds of growth. My 85s strawberries had 84s available by day-3 dawn. Off-by-one-second, costs a full day. Fix: start at t=0 or grant day-1 crops full growth.
7. **Order payouts ignore every price modifier** (difficulty, rep, market mult, hot). Late game they're strictly dominated by warehousing + spike-selling. Run 2 evidence: relaxing my skip rules and honoring big orders caused an **inventory-hostage deadlock** — orders demanding 40+ items reserved my whole warehouse, cash hit single digits for weeks ("Not enough cash for feed!" x14 while sitting on $100k+ of goods), final FV $601k vs $877k. The order system actively punished engagement, −31% on the run.
8. **Heatwave + season boundary = mass extinction with daily watering.** Run 2: back-to-back heatwaves (drain 22s vs a 48s day) on summer d5-d6 stalled tomato regrowth; the ripening slipped past the fall boundary and **83 tomatoes withered in one tick** (toast: "Crop losses: 83 withered out of season"). There is no counterplay at one visit/day — sprinklers also only fire at dawn.
9. **Animal sickness is dead code in practice:** happiness only decays when unfed, so feed-daily = permanently happy = never sick. `vetAnimal` went uncalled for 100 days. The vet, and the 40% vet-bill knob, might as well not exist.
10. **Drone replants without fertilizer** and burns 1 gal/day to replace ~25 free taps. At daily check-ins it's a strictly negative-EV building that exists only as a goal gate (which itself pays +$3,500 via cascade — the only reason to buy one).
11. Not a bug, but a trap: the **Feed Mill** ($2,200) burns wheat (sell value $14+ each) to save $5 chicken feed. Negative EV for coop animals forever.

## 5. Suggestions for 2.0 (ranked, min-max/economy-design lens)

1. **Add an endgame sink / prestige loop.** I had $437k of dead capital by day 60. Sell-the-farm prestige for permanent multipliers (Melvor/idle style), a 10th+ mega-parcel auction with scaling prices, or infrastructure tiers (auto-seller, cargo contracts). Money must always have a next thing.
2. **Flatten the crop table into intended tiers.** Publish (internally) a target $/tile/day band per price tier and re-fit `grow`/`seed`/`base`. Today it's wheat $9 vs cabbage-fert $64 at identical click cost. Every crop should be best at *something* (space efficiency, click efficiency, capital efficiency, order demand).
3. **Fix crafting throughput scaling:** queue-size upgrades, parallel slots, and an "auto-restart last recipe" toggle. The 3-slot queue with >day job times means every $3-6k building converges to 1.5 crafts/day for daily players. This single change would make artisan chains a real alternative to monoculture.
4. **Make orders scale with your price modifiers and add deadline contracts.** Flat 1.6x base loses to spot-market spike-selling by 2x late game. Premium timed contracts (2.5-3x, penalty on miss, capped concurrent) would make the order board the skill-play instead of the skip-button farm.
5. **Scope the greenhouse to an area** (e.g. its own 4x6 interior grid or a radius aura) and let players buy several. Keeps the fantasy, keeps seasons alive, creates a real layout puzzle instead of a global off-switch.
6. **Watering needs an endgame answer that isn't 25 sprinklers:** irrigation pipes/canal network as a buildable infrastructure layer, or a well upgrade that auto-waters owned parcels for a daily fuel/water fee. Also batch the toasts — 1,030 refill notifications is user-hostile.
7. **Add a shovel (remove living crop, partial seed refund) and a "restore grass" tool.** Irreversible tilling + unremovable crops means layout mistakes are permanent and the optimal conversion tool is deliberate drought-murder. That's a QoL hole with a weird moral texture.
8. **Rebalance animals around the sheep benchmark:** sheep→quilt nets ~$113-137/sheep/day; cows ~$17. Either raise product rates/prices (cow to ~2.5x current), give every animal a quilt-grade terminal recipe (truffle→truffle oil!), or cut coop/barn animal costs 50%+. Also make sickness an actual random event so the vet exists.
9. **Give fuel an economy.** Today it's a $3.40/day drone tax with a price ticker nobody needs to watch. Fuel-powered infrastructure (auto-waterers, heaters that prevent heatwave stall, a truck that sells at +15%) would make the daily fuel price a real trading minigame.
10. **Leaderboards + seeded challenges.** Fixed-seed "100-day farm value" runs (my 584x is begging to be beaten), weekly modifier seeds (permanent drought, no fertilizer, animals-only). This game is speedrun-shaped; give it a clock and a board.
11. **Fix the XP-per-call rounding** (`ceil(qty/2)` per sale) and consider making rep XP visible in the sell UI so the rep system reads as a mechanic instead of a hidden passive. The +30% cap at level 31 is unreachable (~350k XP for the final level vs my 100-day level 20); re-curve it.
12. **Drone 2.0:** fertilize option (consumes $20/tile like manual), configurable target crop, upgradeable radius. Today the game's only automation building is worse than doing nothing if you log in daily — automation should extend optimal play, not undercut it.

**Bottom line:** 8/10 engine, 6/10 economy, 3/10 endgame. The optimization puzzle from day 1-25 is legitimately excellent — real math, real routing, no artificial gates. Then the game gets solved and doesn't know it. Give my $437k something to want, make the other 13 crops and 5 animals defensible picks, and cap the tap-tax, and I'll be first on the 2.0 leaderboard you're going to add.
