# Fern's Playtest Journal — Harvest Empire (Cozy, 90 days)

**Final state (Day 90, Summer Year 3):** Level 13 · 125,980 coins · farm value $132,988 · 6 animals · 2 buildings (farmhouse + coop) · 1 land parcel · 2,193 crops harvested · 26 crops lost · 19 orders delivered · 90 in-game days played.

*Who I am: Fern, 34, one toddler and one dog, phone farmer. I play in the 10-minute cracks between laundry cycles and nap time. I picked **Cozy** ($6,000) because the blurb literally said "Relax and build," and that is the entire pitch I needed. Small plot, ~12 tiles. I followed the little goal chip like a good rule-follower, I hoarded everything because selling felt scary, and — full confession — I forgot to water on 19 of my 90 days. Here's how it went.*

---

## The Diary

### Week 1 — Spring, Year 1 (Days 1–10): "Oh, this is nice"
I named the farm Willow Creek and just started tapping. Day 1 I honestly forgot to do much of anything (already off to a great start), but by Day 2 the rain rolled in — "🌧️ Rain today — no watering needed!" — and I got three goal-complete pings in a row: till, plant, coop. I built a coop and a duck named **Pickles** showed up. That little "🦆 Welcome, Pickles!" toast is the moment the game had me.

The goal chip was a fantastic hand-holder early: *Till 4 → Water 4 → Harvest 6 → Collect 5 animal products.* Every one paid me $40–$120 and I felt competent. Day 3 I got a slightly ominous "💾 save a farm backup code" nudge that I ignored (didn't understand it, day 3 felt early to be worried). By Day 8 I completed my first two **orders** for +$215 and +$620 (a RUSH bonus!), which was the biggest money I'd seen, and I leveled to 3. Coins actually *dipped* to ~5,784 by Day 10 because I kept buying chickens and never sold anything — but I had cute animals and a full plot, so I didn't care.

Day 10 the goal chip flipped to **"Place a Scarecrow."** Reader, remember this one.

### Week 2 — Summer, Year 1 (Days 11–20): The rough patch
Summer arrived Day 11 and immediately punched me. "🔥 Heatwave! Crops dry out three times faster." My spring crops "withered out of season" (6 lost) the same day. Then Day 12 I forgot to water during a *second* heatwave and lost 6 more to thirst. Day 16 and Day 20, crows ate crops — each time the toast nagged "Build a Scarecrow to protect your fields." Day 17, another heatwave, another 6 dead of thirst. This was my worst stretch: by Day 21 my loss counter read **19** and I felt like a bad plant-parent.

But — and this matters — Cozy kept catching me. Rush orders on Day 15 (+$420, +$250) refilled my wallet, more birds hatched (Sunny, Buttons, Toffee), and even on my forgetful days the punishment was survivable. I never once feared going broke.

### Week 3 — Fall, Year 1 (Days 21–30): Something magic happened
Fall came Day 21 with one more out-of-season wilt (6 crops), pushing losses to 20, then a couple crow nibbles bringing it to **26**. And then... it just *stopped*. From Day 22 to the end of my entire 90-day run I never lost another crop. Not one.

I didn't understand why at the time, and honestly I still only half-understand it. Starting Day 24 a message began appearing almost every day: **"🌻 The land is at peace — nothing can be disturbed while the flower stands."** There was a sunflower on my plot I hadn't planted, glowing gold, and whenever I tried to tap that tile it just repeated the peace message. I thought it was a bug at first. It turns out (I'm told) it's some rare lucky flower, and it was quietly protecting and even auto-harvesting my whole plot. My harvested count started rocketing — Day 24 my "recent" harvest jumped to 14, Day 25 to 21, Day 27 to 28 — without me doing much. My inventory ballooned to 315 items by Day 30. It felt like the farm was running itself while I did dishes, which for me is the dream. But I want to flag: I got a genuinely powerful thing and the game *never told me what it was.*

### Week 4 — Winter, Year 1 (Days 31–40): I finally sold something
By Day 32 I was sitting on 382 items worth ~$14,950 and roughly 7,800 coins, and I finally worked up the nerve to hit the Market. Day 33 = the best day of the game: coins leapt **7,802 → 31,172**. Cha-CHING. Except the game immediately scolded me: "📉 Dumping 340 Carrot at once crashed its price! It recovers over a few days." So my big triumphant hoard-dump was also me sabotaging my own prices. Nobody had warned me that hoarding-then-dumping was the *wrong* way to sell. Same day I hit "💰 Earn $10,000 lifetime! +$600."

Winter was pretty and calm — snow, cozy, a steady drip of orders ($1,235 on Day 31, $760 on Day 37). The blessed flower kept my plot productive despite frost. I basically coasted.

### Week 5 — Spring, Year 2 (Days 41–50): Rich and confused
More of the same rhythm, and the rhythm was: hoard for ~11 days, dump on the market, watch coins jump. Day 45 my inventory cleared again and coins jumped **33,468 → 63,506**, and I completed "🏦 Reach $60,000 farm value! +$2,000." That was goal #10 — and the *last* goal I ever completed. I was level 10, swimming in cash, and my on-screen goal chip *still* said "Place a Scarecrow." It had said that since Day 10.

### Week 6 — Summer, Year 2 (Days 51–60): Autopilot
This is where I admit the game became a very pleasant screensaver. Day 51 gave me two big rush orders back to back (+$1,165, +$1,195). I'd open the app, feed my six animals ("Fed 6 animals! 🌾" — I saw that toast *so* many times), tap Market when my bag was full, and close it. Coins: 77k. I forgot to water on Day 59 and nothing bad happened, because the flower. There was zero tension left, which for me was 70% blissful and 30% "wait, am I still playing a game?"

### Week 7 — Fall, Year 2 (Days 61–70): The order board vs. me
By now the single most common thing the game said to me was **"📋 order expired — fresh ones are on the way."** I saw that 94 times across the run. I only ever *completed* an order when one happened to match what I already had; I never went hunting for them. So the order board spent 90 days quietly telling me I'd failed at something I wasn't trying to do. Day 66 I grabbed one for +$335. Otherwise the board was just background disappointment.

### Week 8 — Winter, Year 2 (Days 71–80): Comfortable
Snowy, steady, six-figure coins by Day 78 (108k after a dump). Forgot to water Days 72 and 73 — no consequences. Leveled to 13. Farm value cruised past $110k. I owned exactly one parcel and two buildings the whole time and the game never made me feel like I *had* to expand, which I appreciated but also meant I never saw most of the game (buildings, crafting, tractors, drones, the other 8 parcels).

### Week 9 — Spring, Year 3 (Days 81–90): Curtain call
A lovely rainy stretch (Days 82–85, no watering needed — my kind of week). One last order on Day 90 for +$745. I finished Day 90 with **125,980 coins and a $132,988 farm** — more than 20× my starting stake — having never placed the scarecrow the goal chip begged me for since Day 10. Roll credits on my sweet, slightly broken little farm.

---

## What I Loved

1. **The onboarding goal chain (Days 1–10) is genuinely great.** Till → plant → water → harvest → coop → collect → orders, each with a cash reward. On Day 8 I'd completed seven goals and felt like I knew the game. That first hour is the best-designed part.
2. **Rain as free watering is perfect for a forgetful player.** Every "🌧️ Rain today — no watering needed!" (11 of them) felt like the game forgiving me in advance. Spring Year 3 was almost all rain and it was heaven.
3. **Naming animals.** Pickles, Cocoa, Sunny, Buttons, Toffee, Poppy. Tiny thing, huge emotional hook. I would not sell Pickles for any amount of money.
4. **Cozy is genuinely low-stakes.** I forgot to water 19 of 90 days and never came close to failing. For my life, that's the whole value proposition.
5. **The market dump payoff.** Watching coins jump 7,802 → 31,172 on Day 33 after weeks of hoarding was a real dopamine hit, even if I did it "wrong."
6. **Rush orders feel special.** The "⚡ RUSH +25%!" ones (Days 8, 15, 47, 51, 52) were the standout money moments and made me briefly want to engage the order board.
7. **The mystery flower, honestly.** Even confused, finding a golden sunflower that protected my whole farm was a delightful "wait, what is THAT" moment. It's the most memorable thing that happened to me.

## What Confused or Frustrated Me

1. **The goal chip froze on "Place a Scarecrow" from Day 10 to Day 90.** Eighty days pointing at one task I never did. Meanwhile I *did* complete "Earn $10k" (Day 33) and "Reach $60k value" (Day 45) — so real progress was happening, but the chip never reflected it. It made the chip feel broken and useless after the tutorial.
2. **94 "order expired" toasts.** By far the thing the game said to me most. I wasn't chasing orders, so this was 90 days of unsolicited failure notifications. It made a system I was ignoring feel like a system I was *losing at.*
3. **The flower never explained itself.** It protected and auto-harvested my farm — a massive gift — but all I ever got was "the land is at peace" repeated ~58 times whenever I tapped it. I didn't know if it was good, bad, a bug, or something I could lose. Powerful mechanics shouldn't be silent.
4. **Nobody told me hoarding was self-defeating until I'd already done it.** Day 33: "📉 Dumping 340 Carrot at once crashed its price!" Thanks — I'd have loved that warning *before* I built a 340-carrot pile over 30 days.
5. **The early money curve felt like going backwards.** Coins slid from 6,000 (Day 1) down to 4,759 (Day 21) because I was buying animals and hoarding produce. A new player who doesn't sell could easily think they're failing during the exact stretch (Days 11–21) when crops are also dying.
6. **Summer Year 1 difficulty spike.** Four of my six lifetime death-events happened Days 11–21 (heatwaves + season change). It's a real whiplash from the gentle spring tutorial, right when a casual player's habits aren't formed yet.
7. **The "save a backup code" nudge on Day 3** meant nothing to me and I ignored it. Then it never came back, so I have no idea if my farm is actually safe.
8. **Feeding felt like a chore notification.** "Fed 6 animals! 🌾" 69 times. Necessary, but it's the same tap and the same toast every single day.

## Suggestions (at least 15, all tied to my run)

1. **Advance the goal chip past tasks the player skips.** After ~5 in-game days of no progress on "Place a Scarecrow" (which sat there Days 10–90 for me), rotate to a goal I'm actually near — I completed "Earn $10k" and "Reach $60k value" invisibly. Show the goal I'm *closest* to, not a fixed queue position.
2. **Make the goal chip reflect goals completed out of order.** I finished goals #15 and #17 while the chip showed #9. It should celebrate those, not hide them.
3. **Cap or coalesce "order expired" toasts.** I got 94. Roll them into a single quiet daily "3 orders rotated" line, or don't toast expiries at all for players who haven't touched the board in N days.
4. **Introduce the flower.** When the sunflower first spawns (my Day ~24), give it one real explanation toast: what it is, that it protects and auto-harvests, and that harvesting it ends the blessing. The silent poetry is charming once, mysterious for 58 repeats.
5. **Throttle the "land is at peace" lock message harder.** Once I've seen it twice, I understand the tile is locked. After that, just don't respond to the tap, or shake the tile instead of re-toasting.
6. **Warn about market dumping *before* it happens.** When I go to sell a stack over ~50 of one item, pre-empt with "Selling this many at once will crash the price — sell in batches?" I hit the crash on Day 33 with 340 carrots and only learned the rule by breaking it.
7. **Teach selling during the hoard phase.** By Day 20 I had 52 items and $1,840 of unsold produce and declining coins. A gentle "Your barn is filling up — visit the Market to cash in" prompt would have saved my Days 11–21 slump.
8. **Soften the Summer Year-1 spike on Cozy specifically.** Two back-to-back heatwaves (Days 11–12, then 17–19) killed crops on my forgetful days right after a hazard-free spring. On Cozy, maybe no heatwave in the first summer, or a one-time "heatwaves dry crops 3× faster — water before you close" heads-up the first time.
9. **Give a "your crops are thirsty" reminder, ideally a push notification.** I forgot to water 19 days. A mobile nudge ("Willow Creek's crops are dry 🥀") the evening of a dry day would fit the 10-minutes-between-chores play pattern perfectly and is exactly the retention hook this game is missing.
10. **Show net worth trending, not just coins.** During Days 11–21 my coins fell but my farm value *rose* every day (8,364 → 9,447). Surfacing "farm value ↑" would reassure a hoarder that they're still winning while cash dips.
11. **Add a one-tap "sell surplus" / batch-sell button.** My whole economy was hoard-11-days-then-dump. Let me sell everything except what orders need, spread across a few days automatically, so casual players stop crashing their own prices.
12. **Auto-feed option once I own a Feed Mill (or a "feed all" I can set-and-forget).** 69 identical feeding taps over 90 days is the definition of a chore. Let me automate the animals I already own and love.
13. **Explain what buildings/crafting would do for me.** I ended with $126k in cash, one parcel, and two buildings, because nothing ever told me I was leaving 80% of the game on the table. A soft nudge at, say, $30k coins — "You can afford a Creamery — processed goods sell for far more" — would have opened the mid-game up for me.
14. **Re-surface the backup-code prompt when it matters.** The Day 3 nudge was too early and never returned. Remind me around my first big milestone (my Day 33 sale, when I suddenly had 31k to lose) so I actually protect real progress.
15. **Add a tiny end-of-day summary card.** For a 10-minutes-a-day player, a "While you were away / today: +$X earned, N harvested, weather tomorrow: ☀️" card on open would let me reconnect instantly instead of piecing it together from a stack of toasts.
16. **Vary the animal-collection feedback.** Occasionally show total products collected or a milestone ("100 eggs collected! 🥚") instead of the identical "Fed 6 animals" every day, to make the daily loop feel like it's building toward something.
17. **Make orders glanceable and forgiving.** Show a "you already have the goods for this order" highlight on the board so a hoarder like me completes the easy ones. I finished only 19 of the ~113 orders that passed through; most I could probably have filled from my overflowing barn if the game had pointed at them.

## Balance Notes — Was Cozy Actually Cozy?

Mostly, yes — arguably *too* cozy after the opening. My loss ledger tells the whole story: **thirst 12, out-of-season 12, crow 2, and literally zero from rot, storm, or frost.** Every single one of those 26 losses happened in my first 21 days. After Day 21, across 69 more days and 19 forgotten waterings, I lost *nothing*. Part of that is Cozy's gentle weather (eventMult 0.5 — thank you), but a big part was the lucky protective flower carrying me. A more typical Cozy player without that flower would still face thirst/season losses all game, so I'd gently rebalance the *early* window (Days 11–21) down and consider whether the mid-game needs any tension at all — for me it had none.

The coin trajectory was a tale of two games. Days 1–32: flat-to-declining, bottoming at 4,759 on Day 21, because I hoarded and bought animals. Then the market floodgates opened — 31k (Day 33), 63k (Day 45), 77k, 94k, 108k, and 125,980 at the end. That's a 20× return on Cozy while barely engaging half the systems, which says the economy is very forgiving (great for me) but also that hoarding-then-dumping is a dominant, unpunished-enough strategy despite the price-crash mechanic. The crash stung once and I never changed my behavior.

Bottom line: Cozy delivered exactly the relaxing pocket farm it promised, and I'd happily keep it on my phone. It just front-loads all its danger into one scary week and then removes every stake for the remaining eighty days — and it spends those eighty days pointing at a scarecrow I never needed while quietly telling me I'd let 94 orders expire.

---

**Top 3 suggestions (summary):**
1. Fix the goal chip — it froze on "Place a Scarecrow" for 80 of my 90 days while I completed other goals invisibly; show the goal I'm actually closest to.
2. Silence the noise — 94 "order expired" toasts and 58 "land is at peace" repeats made systems I was ignoring feel like failures; coalesce or cap them.
3. Add a water reminder / push nudge — I forgot to water 19 days; an evening "your crops are thirsty" alert is the retention hook a 10-minutes-a-day player needs.
