# June's Playtest Journal — "Slow Sunday" (Cozy, 100 days)

*June, 58. I like a farm I can peek at with my coffee and then forget until the weekend. I picked
**Cozy** because $6,000 and "gentle weather" sounded like a game that wouldn't punish me for having
a life. This is the honest diary of a distractible player.*

---

## 1. Where I ended up

- **Farm:** Slow Sunday (Cozy, ~16-tile plot, seed 5055)
- **Ran:** 100 in-game days, ending Year 3, Fall, Day 101
- **Days I actually showed up:** **52 of 100.** Days I forgot: **48.** I told myself I skip
  "about one day in three" — the data says I skipped **almost one in two.** My longest silent
  stretch was **5 days in a row.**
- **Coins:** started $6,000 → dipped to a low of **$1,360 on Day 31** → ended **$27,577**
- **Level 10**, farm value **$45,317**
- **Still on my starting parcel.** One. The whole game the goal banner read *"Buy your 2nd
  land parcel"* and I never did it. Goals completed: 10.
- **6 animals**, 3 buildings (well, coop, scarecrow). **Zero sprinklers. Zero drones. Fuel: 0,
  every single day.**
- **Crops I killed: 81.** Of those, **59 died of thirst**, 16 withered out of season, 5 were
  eaten by crows, 1 flattened by a storm. That's 13 separate "died of thirst" heartbreaks.
- **363 items sitting unsold in storage ($14,610).** I hoarded. More on that below.

So: I survived, even prospered a little. But I spent 100 days on training wheels and never once got
the self-running farm the box promised. Here's why.

---

## 2. The diary

### Week 1 (Days 1–7) — I forgot the very first day
Classic me: **Day 1 is logged as "forgot."** I made the farm and wandered off. Came back Day 2 to
rain, which felt like the game holding the door open for me. Tilled 14, planted 16, knocked out the
early goals — each dinging a happy "+$40" toast. Genuinely lovely. By **Day 6** I'd built a Coop and
welcomed my first bird. Then **Day 5**, a forgot day, gave my first gut-punch: *"Crop losses: 4 died
of thirst 🥀."* Four little graves because I didn't click a watering can. On Cozy. In spring.

### Week 2 (Days 8–14) — the pattern sets in
**Day 7** and **Day 10**: four more dead of thirst each time, both forgot days. The shape of the
game is already clear — **rain is the only thing standing between me and a graveyard.** I bought
more ducks and fed animals ("Fed # animals! 🌾" — 47 times over the run). Farm value past $8,000.

### Week 3 (Days 15–21) — summer has teeth even on Cozy
**Day 17 was a heatwave AND a day I forgot** — crops dry out *three times faster* in a heatwave,
and four more died of thirst. The "🚧 Buy your 2nd parcel" goal appeared on Day 16 and just… sat
there for the rest of the game. I had the cash; I was always firefighting the farm I had.

### Weeks 4–5 (Days 22–35) — winter, the cash pinch, and the massacre
Fall was my richest stretch — "recent income" hit $14–15/day and inventory swelled. But I never
*sold* much, so on **Day 31, the first day of winter, my coins bottomed out at $1,360** — thousands
of dollars of crops in storage, almost no cash, because "hoard" is my natural strategy and the game
let me. Then **Day 33 — my worst day.** A forgot day, deep winter, a big field, and I came back to
*"Crop losses: 12 died of thirst 🥀."* **Twelve** in one notice — I'd assumed winter was *safer* to
neglect and instead lost my biggest harvest. **Days 35 and 38** took four more each. Winter was
where neglect hurt most.

### Weeks 6–7 (Days 36–50) — Year 2, comfortable but stagnant
Year 2 opened rich: by **Day 43 I had $11,038.** Around the season flip on **Day 41** I got a new
flavor of loss — *"4 withered out of season"* then *"8 withered out of season"* — crops I'd left
in the ground didn't match the new season and slowly died. It felt like a different, quieter death
than thirst, and I didn't understand it at first. I *did* buy the **Hose Cart** (bigger can,
2-tile reach) — helpful on the days I show up, useless on the days I don't.

### Weeks 8–11 (Days 51–80) — the treadmill
This stretch tells the real story. The Year 2 thirst deaths: **Day 61, 66, 74, 78, 81 — four crops
each, every time on a day I forgot.** Same wound, roughly once a week, for the entire back half of
the game. I had **$13,000+ in the bank** the whole time — I could trivially have bought a sprinkler
($600) or the drone ($7,500). But nothing put that choice in front of me while I was bleeding crops,
so I kept re-watering by hand and losing four a week. Meanwhile *"📋 order expired"* toasts piled up
— **96 of them** — because I could never keep up with the board.

### Weeks 12–14 (Days 81–100) — a quiet, slightly hollow finish
The last stretch was calm; rain covered a lot of my absences. Coins climbed to $27,577. I ended
with **363 items and $14,610 of stuff I never sold**, six animals I loved (the naming — "🦆
Welcome, Biscuit!" — is genuinely charming), and a farm that looked exactly like it did on Day 20,
just with more money in the mattress. The emotional arc wasn't stress — Cozy kept me solvent — it
was a low background **guilt**: every time I came back, some small thing had died, and I never
built the one thing that would've stopped it.

---

## 3. What I loved

1. **Rain as a free pass.** *"🌧️ Rain today — no watering needed!"* (17 times) is the single most
   forgiving mechanic in the game. On rainy forgot-days I lost nothing — exactly the "the world
   watered your farm for you" grace an idler wants.
2. **Cozy really is solvent-proof.** Even at my $1,360 low on Day 31 I was never near failing, never
   had to sell an animal for rent. For a nervous player that safety net matters enormously.
3. **The onboarding goals are perfect.** Till 4, plant 4, water 4, build a coop — each a tiny "+$40"
   hit. Week one felt guided and warm without ever nagging.
4. **Animals are pure joy and low-maintenance.** Feeding is one tap, they don't die if I miss a day,
   and the names (Clover, Cocoa, Mochi, Toffee, Biscuit) gave me something to care about. My 6
   animals were my most *reliable* income.
5. **The batched death notice is humane.** *"Crop losses: 12 died of thirst"* in one line instead
   of twelve toasts — small mercy, big difference.
6. **The forecast and status toasts** meant that when I *did* play, I always knew what was going on
   at a glance. Moment-to-moment play is genuinely relaxing.

---

## 4. What confused or frustrated me

1. **Thirst is a slow, repeated bleed I could never stop by hand.** 59 dead across **13 events**,
   roughly one every week or two, start to finish. Never catastrophic on Cozy — usually just 4 —
   but it *never goes away*, and for a player whose whole pitch is "I forget to water," that's the
   core loop quietly failing me.
2. **I never got automation, and the game never nudged me toward it.** The big one. I ended with
   $27,577 and **zero sprinklers, zero drones.** The one feature that makes neglect *safe* — the
   thing the README calls "the goal" — I sailed past for 100 days because nothing surfaced it when
   I was actually losing crops.
3. **The sprinkler is too small and too invisible.** It covers only **3×3 (9 tiles)** for $600, so
   my ~16-tile plot needs *two*, and neither appears near the moment crops are dying. I'd never
   have guessed that from play.
4. **The drone runs on fuel, and mine was 0 all game.** Even had I bought the $7,500 drone, it
   burns a gallon a day and grounds itself empty. Buying diesel is exactly the recurring chore an
   idler forgets — the same forgetting that killed my crops would strand my drone.
5. **Winter fooled me.** I assumed cold = safe. Instead Day 33 gave my worst loss (12 dead).
   Moisture lasts longer in winter, but 1.5 days of wilt is still 1.5 days, and a 5-day absence
   blows through it.
6. **"Out of season" deaths were a mystery.** *"4 withered out of season"* on the Day 41 flip read
   like a bug until I thought about it. Nothing warned me my spring crops were doomed when fall came.
7. **96 expired orders.** The board clearly pays well, but a twice-a-week player can never fill
   anything in time — so it's just a stream of failure toasts I couldn't participate in.
8. **I accidentally hoarded $14,610 of goods.** Nothing sells itself, so "recent income" looked
   healthy while my coins sat low (the Day 31 pinch). An idler won't remember to visit the market.
9. **Coming back, I had to reconstruct what happened myself** — just the same loss toast I'd have
   seen live, no "here's what died and here's the one fix."

---

## 5. Suggestions (weighted for idlers, automation, and coming back)

1. **Make "leaving the tab open" behave like being offline.** The kind offline fast-forward
   (no thirst, no wilt, crops finish and wait) is lovely — but I lost 59 crops because *forgetting
   with the game still running* uses the harsh live rules. Idlers leave tabs open. After, say, 10
   minutes of no input, switch a live session into the same gentle "away" mode.
2. **Surface automation exactly when I'm bleeding crops.** The third or fourth "died of thirst"
   toast should carry a button: *"Tired of this? A Sprinkler ($600) waters this field every
   morning."* I had the money 13 times over and never saw the offer.
3. **Sell me a sprinkler that actually covers a plot.** A 3×3 that needs two units for a 16-tile
   field is a puzzle I didn't know I was solving. Offer a bigger-radius "Irrigation Line," or let
   one sprinkler cover a whole parcel, so *one* affordable purchase ends thirst deaths.
4. **Give me a fuel-free automation tier.** A drone I have to keep gassing up will strand itself
   the same way I strand my crops. A solar sprinkler or electric drone (higher upfront cost, no
   fuel) is the honest idle-friendly option — my fuel was literally 0 for 100 days.
5. **Auto-buy fuel / auto-sell options.** Let me tick "keep the tank topped up from my coins," and
   an opt-in "auto-sell surplus over N" — that would have spent my $14,610 hoard and kept cash healthy.
6. **A real welcome-back screen.** On return, one card: *"While you were away (5 days): 12 crops
   died of thirst, 6 harvests waiting, 3 orders expired,"* with one-tap *"Water everything"* and
   *"Fix this so it stops"* (build sprinkler) buttons.
7. **Stretch WILT_DAYS on Cozy specifically.** 1.5 days of drought-survival is tuned for a daily
   player. Cozy's audience is people like me — let bone-dry crops survive ~3 days so one missed day
   never kills.
8. **Warn me before season-flip deaths, loudly.** On a season's last day: *"Fall ends tomorrow — 8
   crops won't survive it."* The quiet "withered out of season" losses caught me twice.
9. **A gentle opt-in push/email reminder** when the farm needs me. A tap on the shoulder *before*
   the graveyard is what would help an idler most.
10. **Auto-extend automation to new fields.** When I till a new patch, offer to cover it with my
    existing watering/scarecrow, so expanding doesn't silently create dry, unprotected tiles.
11. **Make the order board skippable without noise.** 96 "expired" toasts is 96 tiny failures — let
    me hide the board or fill orders passively from stock.
12. **Explain winter's real risk.** A one-time tip that cold drains slowly but a long absence still
    kills. I wrongly felt safe and lost my biggest field on Day 33.
13. **Bigger text and a calmer, scrollable toast log for older eyes.** Feed/refill/loss/order toasts
    pile up fast; I couldn't always read what happened.
14. **Repeat the backup-code nudge as one tap.** I saw it exactly once. For a casual who might clear
    their browser, that lifeline should reappear and be a single button.
15. **A true "vacation mode" toggle** — one switch that pauses thirst, auto-waters and auto-feeds
    while I'm away. It fits Cozy perfectly.
16. **Auto-replant without a drone.** A cheap "keep this field planted with X" would let me
    set-and-forget instead of re-tilling every return.
17. **Show the automation goal early.** The "🤖 Automate" goal is buried at #15 — move a "water your
    whole farm automatically" milestone into week one so every idler knows the escape hatch exists.

---

## 6. Balance analysis — is Cozy forgiving enough for a true idler?

**Half-and-half.** On the axis that scares casual players — *going broke* — Cozy is a triumph. I
skipped **48 of 100 days**, hoarded instead of selling, ignored the order board, and *still* my
worst moment was a temporary $1,360 dip before climbing to $27,577. I was never at risk of losing
the farm. For financial survival, Cozy is exactly as forgiving as it promises.

But on the axis that defines *my* play style — *neglect without loss* — Cozy is only half-there.
**59 thirst deaths across 13 events, for the entire game** is the signature of a system tuned for a
daily player and handed to a twice-a-week one. The losses never broke me, but they kept a low guilt
humming throughout. The forgiveness comes from *rain* (17 free passes) and *Cozy's fat wallet*, not
from any system designed to let me be absent.

**And automation — the actual answer — was never reachable in practice, not for cost but for
discovery.** A sprinkler is $600 (had it by Day 6); the drone is $7,500 (had it by Day 40).
Affordability was never the gate — the game just never connected "you keep losing crops to thirst"
to "here's the $600 machine that fixes it," so I limped through 100 days on a watering can. And even
if I'd bought in, the sprinkler's 3×3 footprint and the drone's daily fuel appetite are both quietly
hostile to the exact player automation is *for*.

So: Cozy keeps an idler *solvent*, but it doesn't keep an idler's *farm* alive on its own, nor guide
them to the tools that would. Surface automation at the moment of pain, make one affordable purchase
cover a plot, and drop the fuel chore — and Cozy becomes the genuinely self-running farm the idle
audience came for.

---

**Top 3 suggestions:**
1. Surface automation at the moment I'm losing crops — a "died of thirst" toast should offer the $600 sprinkler that stops it, because affordability was never my problem, discovery was.
2. Make one affordable purchase actually cover a whole plot, and offer a fuel-free automation tier, so the sprinkler/drone stop being hostile to the very idler they're built for.
3. Treat an open-but-idle tab like being offline (no thirst/wilt) and add a real welcome-back screen with a one-tap "water everything" — that alone erases most of my 59 thirst deaths.
