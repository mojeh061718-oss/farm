# Harvest Empire — Playtest Report
**Persona:** Marisol, 34 — cozy-game veteran (800 hrs Stardew, Animal Crossing fan). Cozy difficulty, 100 in-game days at "Buttercup Hollow".
**Run result:** 0 JS errors. Final: $33,098 cash, $64,744 farm value, level 12, 8 animals (0 ever sick), 803 harvests, 14 crops lost (every one to forced season-end withering), 9 orders, 4 artisan goods.

---

## 1. Weekly diary

**Week 1 (Spring, Y1)** — What a lovely start! $6,000 nest egg meant my scarecrow went up on day 1 (no crow will ever make me cry), the coop on day 2 with Toffee the chicken, then Pepper the duck, the barn, Maple the cow and Waffles the goat — one new friend almost every day. Delivered two orders on day 3 for $130 and $305. Ended the week with $1,740 and a heart very full.

**Week 2 (Summer, Y1)** — Adopted a sheep... and the game named her Toffee. I already HAVE a Toffee. Survived my first heatwave and my first storm (scarecrow held the line, zero damage) — but when fall arrived, 7 of my tomato and pepper bushes just withered overnight and there was nothing I could do. Down to $493.

**Week 3 (Fall→Winter, Y1)** — The scary week. After buying Toffee-the-sheep I hit $254 on day 20, and on winter day 3 the game told me "Not enough cash for seeds" three times when I tried to plant $90 frostberries. Two more crops withered when winter came. First kale though!

**Week 4 (Winter→Spring, Y2)** — Recovery! First frostberry harvest (so pretty), a convenient $110 order, and spring brought the coins back: $2,811. Nobody got sick because I feed everyone every single morning.

**Week 5 (Spring→Summer, Y2)** — A $400 order, another heatwave survived, and on day 35 — finally — Clover the pig! That's one of every animal, my forever tradition, complete. It took 35 days to afford her, which felt like forever.

**Week 6 (Fall, Y2)** — Bought the meadow next door (that's where the greenhouse will go someday). Clover dug up her first truffle — worth $265, my clever girl! Two more bushes withered at the season change, which I'm starting to dread. $3,506 saved.

**Week 7 (Winter, Y2)** — A quiet snowy week of kale, garlic and frostberries. The order board only asks for enormous quantities now, so I just ignore it. $6,845 in the greenhouse fund.

**Week 8 (Spring→Summer, Y3)** — Head down, watering can up. Reputation level 10. $8,976. My morning routine is: harvest, feed six babies, water 15 plots, refill the can three times. Every. Single. Day.

**Week 9 (Summer→Fall, Y3)** — $12,673! The greenhouse money is IN HAND. Two more crops withered at the fall changeover, and I whispered "never again" to them.

**Week 10 (Fall→Winter, Y3)** — DAY 64: THE GREENHOUSE IS REAL. $12,000 gone, $1,753 left, zero regrets. Also squeezed in the bakery on day 69 and adopted Ginger the chicken. Strawberries in winter, here I come.

**Week 11 (Winter Y3→Spring Y4)** — Poppy the duck makes 8 animals, and I baked my first-ever Berry Cake and Pumpkin Pie in the same week. Best week of the whole run. $10,246.

**Week 12 (Spring→Summer, Y4)** — Three goals popped in one morning (crafting, 8 animals, $10,000 lifetime: +$1,300). But the baking stopped — my field is now all strawberry, tomato and grape bushes that never leave, so no wheat ever gets planted, so no bread. And I can't dig a healthy bush up! $18,185.

**Week 13 (Fall, Y4)** — Coasting. 76 harvests this week, all from the berry jungle. $25,651 and genuinely nothing left I want to buy — the only thing the game suggests is a $7,500 robot drone, which, no thank you.

**Week 14 (Winter Y4→Spring Y5)** — Passed $60,000 farm value and level 12. Winter doesn't even scare us anymore; the greenhouse keeps the frost off everything. $31,665.

**Week 15 (Spring, Y5)** — Day 100. $33,098, eight healthy well-fed animals who have never seen a vet, 803 lifetime harvests, and a farm I'm proud of but can't decorate. Four whole years flew by. I'd play again tomorrow... if there were a pet dog waiting.

## 2. What I LOVED

1. **Cozy difficulty is actually cozy.** The $6,000 start meant the scarecrow went up on day 1 and the coop on day 2 — I never had to grind before getting my animals. Halved event rates meant zero disasters felt unfair.
2. **The scarecrow is protection done right.** One $350 build covered my whole field against crows AND storms: 4 storms and ~30 sunny "crow days" over 100 days, and I lost literally nothing to either. Prevention as a purchase, not a punishment — my favorite design in the game.
3. **The animal names.** Toffee, Pepper, Maple, Waffles, Clover, Ginger, Poppy — with a little "Welcome, Maple!" toast each time. Instant attachment.
4. **No death spirals.** Free well refills, free odd-jobs as an income floor, buildings sell back at 50%. Even at my $254 low point I never felt doomed.
5. **Rain days are days off.** "🌧️ Rain today — no watering needed!" is the coziest toast in the game. I got 18 of them and loved every one.
6. **Price is the only gate.** No level requirements on anything — my play order (coop → barn → one of each → greenhouse) was fully respected.
7. **Collection joy.** All 18 crops and all 6 animal products harvested by early Year 2, each with a little first-time thrill (frostberries! truffles!). Multi-harvest berry bushes feel wonderfully generous.
8. **Kind systemic touches:** crop deaths arrive as one batched message instead of toast spam, offline decay runs at half speed, and the save system has rotating backups. It never once errored (0 JS errors in 100 days).

## 3. What I HATED / frustrated me

1. **There is no shovel.** You cannot remove a living crop — only a dead one. Every single season change, my healthy strawberry/tomato/grape bushes were sentenced to wither overnight ("Crop losses: 7 withered out of season 🍂") and all I could do was watch. ALL 14 of my lifetime losses were this, and every one was unavoidable. For a player whose whole identity is "never let anything die," this is the worst feeling in the game.
2. **Morning watering is a chore.** The starter can holds 6 water; my modest 15-tile field needed 3 refills every morning — 178 "Watering can refilled!" toasts over the run (three of them are stacked on my final screenshot). And you can't place a sprinkler on tilled soil, so retrofitting relief means sacrificing crop beds you already dug.
3. **The order board abandoned me.** I filled 9 orders in the first 36 days, then ZERO for the remaining 64 — quantities scale with lifetime earnings, so a casual seller gets asked for 5–12 each of up to 3 items. My favorite early activity became dead content.
4. **The goal list dead-ends at a robot.** "Automate: drone + sprinkler (0/2)" has been pinned to the top of my screen since day 79. I will never buy a $7,500 drone; it's the opposite of why I play. Behind it sit goals I DID effectively complete ($60k farm value) that I'll never get credit for.
5. **Nothing to spend love (or money) on.** No decorations, no fences or flowers, no renaming animals, no petting, no pet dog. I finished with $33,098 and the game had nothing cozy left to sell me after day ~70.
6. **The Year 1 winter wall.** Following the intended "buy the animals" fantasy on COZY dropped me to $254 by day 20, and the game answered my empty seed purse with a tip to go do day labor in town. Frostberry seeds are $90 in the season when income is thinnest.
7. **Time pressure never fully goes away.** 48-second days and 6-day seasons mean ripe crops rot in ~2 days and dry crops die in 1.5 — I could never truly skip a morning, which is exactly the leash I play cozy games to escape.

## 4. Bugs & oddities (with evidence)

1. **Duplicate animal names.** Chicken "Toffee" (day 2) and sheep "Toffee" (day 12) — names are picked randomly with replacement from a 20-name list (`ANIMAL_NAMES` in data.js; `pick()` in `buyAnimal`). Telemetry toasts show two separate "Welcome, Toffee!" events.
2. **No API/UI action removes a living crop** — `clearDead` is the only clear. Combined with regrow crops, this guarantees "lost" stat increments at season changes. Evidence: all 14 losses were "withered out of season"; 0 to thirst/rot/crows/storms.
3. **The greenhouse is a global magic flag, not a place.** `seasonOK()` and the frost event just check `hasBuilding('greenhouse')` — one 2×2 building makes every outdoor tile farm-wide frost-proof and season-free. You never plant inside it. Cozy-friendly but very strange fiction.
4. **Greenhouse + regrow crops = immortal bush lock-in.** Post-greenhouse, `harvest()` always keeps regrow crops (seasonOK is always true), so the field converged to permanent strawberry/tomato/grape/pepper bushes. My planting rate collapsed from ~7/day to ~0.3/day after day 64 (telemetry: `stats.planted` 414→466 over 36 days), which starved the bakery of wheat/pumpkin — `stats.crafted` froze at 4 from day 80 to day 100. Without a shovel, there is NO way to rotate the field back.
5. **Goals are strictly sequential, so credit arrives late or never.** I placed the scarecrow on day 1; the "Place a Scarecrow" goal paid out on day ~15 when the queue reached it. "Reach $60,000 farm value" (achieved, $64,744) can never trigger because it sits behind the drone goal I'll never do. `goalIndex` pinned at 14 from day 79 onward.
6. **Order scaling kills the board for casual players.** `makeOrder()` wealth factor = 1 + min(6, earned/4000); at my $77k lifetime earnings, orders ask ~2.45× base quantities across up to 3 items. Data: 9 orders fulfilled before day 36, 0 after.
7. **"Not enough cash for seeds!" toast fired 3× in one morning** (winter Y1 day 3) — the out-of-season warning has an anti-spam cooldown but the broke-toast doesn't.
8. **Genuinely clean tech:** 0 page errors, 0 console errors, no NaNs or negative values anywhere in 100 days of telemetry. Save/backup layering looks solid.

## 5. Suggestions for 2.0 (ranked, from a cozy player)

1. **Add a shovel/trowel** to dig up living crops (partial seed refund). Ends the forced season-end deaths AND fixes the immortal-bush field lock-in. My #1 by a mile.
2. **Decorations and cozy money sinks:** fences, flower beds, paths, ponds, benches, a farmhouse to furnish. I ended with $33k and an un-decoratable farm.
3. **Let me name (and rename) my animals**, prevent duplicate auto-names, and add a pet interaction with a little heart. A farm dog or cat that follows you would sell the game on its own.
4. **Fix late-game orders:** scale to recent sales rather than lifetime earnings, or add a permanent "small favors" board (2–4 items) alongside the big contracts.
5. **Branch the goal track:** parallel Rancher / Baker / Gardener paths and skippable goals, so cozy players aren't nagged forever by a drone they'll never buy — and can still earn the milestones they actually reach.
6. **Watering QoL floor:** starter can holds 10+, or the well passively waters adjacent tiles, and let sprinklers be placed on tilled soil.
7. **A plantable greenhouse interior** (its own little grid) instead of a farm-wide magic aura — more fiction, more coziness, and it naturally limits the immortal-bush problem.
8. **Season-transition care screen:** the evening before a season flips, show "these 4 plants won't survive spring→summer" with one-tap harvest-and-clear.
9. **A "Slow Living" clock option for Cozy:** longer days/seasons, rot timers doubled, or crops pause while menus are open.
10. **Smooth the Year-1 animal curve on Cozy:** cheaper sheep/pig or a starter-animal discount, so completing "one of each" doesn't require a $254 near-bankruptcy winter.

## 6. Balance notes (numbers from my run)

- **Winter Y1 wall:** low of $254 on day 20 on COZY while following the sanctioned pet-collector path (sheep $1,900 + pig $3,200 vs early income of ~$150–300/day). The pig took until day 35; frostberry seeds cost $90 in the poorest season.
- **Greenhouse at $12,000 took 64 days (2.7 in-game years)** with a modest 15-tile field. As the definitive cozy dream purchase, that's a long grind — $8k, or a mid-tier "cold frame" ($4k, frost protection only), would land better.
- **Fertilizer $20/use adds up invisibly:** 192 uses = $3,840 lifetime — more than my well + scarecrow + coop + barn combined. A bulk bag or upgrade would feel kinder.
- **Order inflation:** wealth multiplier hits its 6× cap at $24k lifetime earned; result was 9 orders in days 1–36, 0 in days 37–100.
- **Regrow crops are strictly dominant post-greenhouse:** grapes yield $88 base every ~65s regrow with zero replant cost; planting fell from ~7/day to ~0.3/day. Single-harvest crops (and thus the bakery's wheat) can't compete.
- **Reputation is nearly flavor:** level 12 after 100 days and 803 harvests = +11% sell bonus; the +30% cap needs level 31, which the exponential XP curve (30·L·1.22^L) puts out of reach.
- **Crow/storm pressure on Cozy is ~zero with one scarecrow** (0 losses in 100 days across 4 storms). Honestly correct for the audience — but then the crow warning toast copy could stop threatening me.
- **Animal economics are pleasantly safe:** 8 animals ≈ $90/day feed vs ~$300+/day products (a single $265 truffle beats the whole feed bill). Good — keep it.
- **Real-time pacing:** 48s days mean a season is under 5 minutes and a year is ~19 minutes; wilt (1.5d) and rot (2d) windows are ~72–96 real seconds of buffer. That's tight for a game that sells itself as relaxing.
