# Milo's Harvest Empire Playtest Journal — "Touch Everything"

**Tester:** Milo, 22, completionist. **Difficulty:** Classic ($3,000 start). **Plot:** ~18 tiles, good diligence. **Length:** 100 in-game days (2.5 game-years). Farm name: *Wanderlust Farm*.

My goal was simple and a little unhinged: **touch every single feature.** Every crop, every building, every recipe, fertilizer, the drone, sprinklers, all six animals, land expansion — and, because I'm me, plant things wildly out of season just to see what happens. Spoiler: a *lot* of dead crops.

---

## 1. Final State (from the run's `final` snapshot)

- **Day 101, Year 3, Fall** (ended on a storm, fittingly)
- **Coins:** $48,614
- **Farm value:** $114,591
- **Reputation:** Level 14 (of a possible 21; sell bonus was still climbing)
- **Land:** 5 of 9 parcels
- **Buildings:** 11
- **Animals:** 12 (a full Coop + a full Barn)
- **Goals:** 17 of 18 complete — only *"Own the whole valley"* left
- **Lifetime totals:** earned **$149,686**, sold **2,098** goods, harvested **971** crops, crafted **104** artisan goods, collected **890** animal products, fertilized **214** crops, planted **518** seeds… and **lost 343 crops.**
- **Cause of death, all 343:** `season` **292**, `thirst` 24, `crow` 16, `storm` 11, `rot` 0, `frost` 0.

Read that last line twice. **85% of everything I lost died because I planted it out of season.** That's the whole story of my run, and it's the thing I most want the team to read about. More below.

## 2. The Diary

### Week 1 (Days 1–7, Spring Y1) — fumbling the opening
Days **1 and 2** are logged as `forgot` — it rained both days ("🌧️ Rain today — no watering needed!") and I didn't do anything productive. Coins sat at $3,000, nothing tilled. The game didn't push me; I just didn't know where to start.

**Day 3** I woke up: tilled 16 tiles, planted 18 crops, and — being a completionist — immediately fertilized 10 just to try the ✨ button. Cleared three goals in a burst (Till, Plant, Water) and built a **Coop**. Coins cratered $3,000→$994; I overspent early and felt it. **Day 4:** first harvest (18), first sales, **$759 earned**, Level 3. **Day 5:** first chicken, plus upgrades to the **Hose Cart** and **Rototiller** (both tier-1 — I never went higher, more on that later). By **Day 7**, 3 animals and $2,556 lifetime.

### Week 2 (Days 8–14, Spring→Summer) — the scarecrow that took forever
On **Day 8** the goal *"Place a Scarecrow"* appeared. I didn't place one until **Day 19** — eleven days staring at the same unmet card while crows nibbled me ("🐦‍⬛ Crows ate a crop!" fires 16 times across the run). **Day 9:** **Barn**. **Day 11** flipped to Summer with a storm; I doubled planting to 36 tiles and logged my **first losses** (0→3). Animals reached 6.

### Week 3 (Days 15–21, Summer→Fall) — infrastructure spree
**Day 15:** **Sprinkler** (my first automation — waters a 3×3 at dawn). Herd up to 9. **Day 19:** finally the **Scarecrow**, plus a new goal, *"Craft 4 artisan goods,"* which needed a processing building I didn't own. **Day 21** (Fall) my herd hit 12 — full Coop (6) + full Barn (6) — and stayed there all game.

### Week 4 (Days 22–28, Fall Y1) — the production chain opens up
**Day 22** is a `forgot` day. **Day 24:** the **Bakery**, my first processing building and my favorite part of the game. **Day 27:** **Creamery**. **Day 28:** my first craft (`crafted` → 1) — it took until Day 28 to make one artisan good despite the goal appearing Day 19, purely because I had to save for the $3,200 Bakery.

### Week 5 (Days 29–35, Fall→Winter) — juice, then the freeze
**Day 30:** **Juice Press**. **Day 31** dropped into Winter (snow); I tilled up to 39 tiles and kept planting. My `frost` death count is **0** for the entire game — I planted winter-hardy kale/frostberry, so the losses I racked up were out-of-season deaths, not frost. Winter Y1 was my richest stretch, hitting **$12,361** by Day 35.

### Week 6 (Days 36–42, Winter→Spring Y2) — everything automated
**Day 37:** **Loom House** (wool → cloth → quilt, the juiciest value chain). **Day 38:** **Greenhouse.** **Day 48:** **Harvest Drone**, completing *"Automate: drone + sprinkler"* for **+$500**. By now: 11 buildings, 4 parcels, farm value past $58,000.

And here's the completionist's shame: **I never built the Feed Mill** — not once, in a run designed to touch every building. Which is why my single most common toast is **"Fed # animals! 🌾" — 166 times** — every one me *paying cash* to feed animals the Feed Mill would have fed free by grinding my surplus wheat and corn. I even hit "Not enough cash for feed!" five times. I owned the grain. I owned a grinder-shaped hole in my empire.

### Weeks 7–14 (Days 49–100, Y2 Summer → Y3 Summer) — the plateau and the carnage
From **Day 48 onward my building count froze at 11.** I'd bought one of everything I *thought* to buy, and the rest of the run was a steady-state loop: harvest, craft, sell, buy the occasional parcel. Parcels crept from 4 (Day 41) to 5 (Day 80) and stopped; reputation ground from Level 11 to 14; coins recovered to $48k.

The real narrative of the back half is the **out-of-season death curve**, and it is relentless:

| Day | Total crops lost |
|---|---|
| 41 | 31 |
| 51 | 96 |

That **155→237 jump between Days 70 and 80** is Y2 Winter, when I got cocky and drag-planted rows of summer/fall crops "to see if the greenhouse would save them." It did not — **a Greenhouse only shelters a 6×6 zone, and I owned exactly one** on a farm across five parcels. Everything outside that patch withered by morning.

`forgot` days: **1, 2, 22, 29, 36, 47, 58, 96, 99** — nine idle days. Credit where due: the offline protection is real. Nothing wilted or rotted on those days; every one of my 343 deaths happened while I was *actively playing.*

## 3. What I Loved

1. **The production chain is deep and satisfying.** I crafted **104 artisan goods**, and the wool → Fine Cloth → Wool Quilt ladder ($150 → $650 → $1,500) is a real "numbers go up" hit. Turning milk into cheese/butter/yogurt and fruit into premium juice made me feel like I ran a business, not a garden.
2. **No unlock gates, ever.** Buying the Bakery, Creamery, Press and Loom the moment I could afford them — no "reach Level 8 first" wall — is exactly right. Money is the only ladder and I respect it.
3. **Fertilizer is a no-brainer I happily spammed** — **214 times.** +25% growth and a 45%-vs-8% double-harvest chance is a clean, generous deal.
4. **The goal arc is a great tour guide.** Being nudged toward my first Coop, order, craft, and automation gave my chaos a spine; *Automate: drone + sprinkler* for +$500 on Day 48 was a highlight.
5. **Automation feels like a real endgame.** The drone auto-harvesting *and replanting* a 5×5 (and sensibly refusing to replant out of season) plus dawn sprinklers turned a corner of my farm into a printer. "🤖 Drones harvested # crops!" fired 38 times.
6. **Offline forgiveness is kind.** Nine idle days, zero punishment — the right call for a dip-in-and-out browser game.
7. **Seasons genuinely change the game.** Swapping crop rosters and palettes kept me re-planning, and weather with teeth (heatwaves, storms, crows) made the world feel alive.

## 4. What Confused or Frustrated Me

1. **The out-of-season carnage — 292 of 343 losses — was catastrophically under-telegraphed.** The *only* warning is one transient toast at plant time: *"⚠️ [Crop] is out of season — it will wither and the seed money is gone!"* — and it's rate-limited to suppress repeats for 12 seconds so it won't spam during drag-planting. So drag-planting 9 tomatoes gave me **one** fleeting toast, then next morning *"Crop losses: 9 withered out of season 🍂."* That exact 9-die pattern repeats over and over in my log. No persistent tile marker, no gray-out in the seed menu, no confirmation — a toast gone in two seconds and a field dead by dawn.
2. **Out-of-season crops die faster than thirsty ones — deeply counterintuitive.** In the sim, a dry crop takes 1.5 days to wilt; an out-of-season crop wilts in **0.8 days**, under one day. The "wasted seed money" framing badly undersells it: these aren't slow fizzles, they're the *quickest* death in the game. Nothing told me that.
3. **The Greenhouse's coverage is invisible and tiny.** I assumed "grows any crop in any season" meant my whole farm. It's a **6×6 zone around one 2×2 building.** The description says "Build several!" but with no on-map footprint overlay, I planted experiments all over and watched them die *next to* a greenhouse guarding only a sliver. This one misunderstanding likely drove most of my 292 season deaths.
4. **The Feed Mill's value is non-obvious, so I skipped the building that would've paid back most.** Nothing said "you've paid to feed 166 times — grind wheat for 3 free feeds per grain." A completionist who *forgets a whole building* is a discoverability failure, not a player failure.
5. **The Scarecrow goal sat unmet 11 days (Day 8 → 19)** with no hint where to get one or why it mattered, while crows ate 16 crops. The goal named the fix to my crow problem but never connected the two.
6. **Recipe throughput is a black box.** I crafted 104 goods but never had a clear read on how many craft lanes I had, how long a recipe blocked a building, or whether extra slots (I never bought any) were worth it.
7. **The mid-game plateau.** Buildings froze at 11 and goals at 17/18, both on Day 48. The last ~50 days had nothing *new* to reach for but cash toward parcels I couldn't quite afford.
8. **Land ran out of reach.** *"Own the whole valley"* wants all 9 parcels; the last cost $32k/$45k/$60k. I ended with $48k and 5 parcels — the final goal was mathematically off the table for a 100-day Classic run, hanging over me permanently incomplete.
9. **I never left tool tier 1.** My upgrades stopped at Rototiller/Hose Cart on Day 5; the shop never resurfaced the Compact Tractor or Irrigation Rig as I scaled, so I forgot they existed.

## 5. Suggestions (concrete, ordered roughly by impact)

1. **Persist the out-of-season warning on the tile, not in a toast.** A doomed crop should render with a wilting/red tint and a small 🍂 badge from the moment it's planted, so a drag-planted row of 9 shows 9 warnings, not one that already vanished.
2. **Add a plant-time confirmation for out-of-season seeds** ("Plant Tomato out of season? It will die within a day and the $48 seed is lost." — Cancel / Plant Anyway), at least until the player has lost N crops this way. My 292 deaths would've been maybe 20.
3. **Gray out / flag out-of-season crops in the seed-buy menu** with a "🍂 not in season (dies fast)" label and their in-season list. Let me *see* what's plantable before I spend.
4. **Fix the framing: it's not "wasted seed money," it's "dies in under a day."** The current copy makes it sound like a slow fizzle. Say what actually happens.
5. **Draw the Greenhouse coverage footprint on the map** — a translucent 6×6 tint when a greenhouse is selected or when I'm holding the plant tool, exactly like scarecrow range should show too. Coverage you can't see is coverage you'll plant outside of.
6. **Surface the Greenhouse "build several" message where it matters.** When I plant out of season *near but outside* a greenhouse, the toast should say "This tile isn't inside a Greenhouse's 6×6 zone — build another to cover it."
7. **Teach the Feed Mill contextually.** After I've paid cash to feed animals ~10 times while holding surplus grain, fire a one-time tip: "You're spending coins to feed. A Feed Mill turns 1 wheat/corn into 3 free feeds." I'd have built it instantly.
8. **Add a persistent "Buildings you don't own yet" panel** — a checklist of the 12 building types with cost and a one-line "why you'd want it." Completionists live for that checklist, and it would've caught my missing Feed Mill on sight.
9. **Show recipe throughput in the building panel:** craft lanes in use (e.g. "2 / 3 lanes"), time remaining per lane, and a clear "Buy extra lane — $2,000" prompt with the payoff spelled out.
10. **Connect goals to their solutions.** The Scarecrow goal card should link straight to the shop entry ("Place a Scarecrow — buy for $350"), and ideally surface after the first crow attack, not before.
11. **Re-advertise tool upgrades as I scale.** When my tilled/watered tile count crosses a threshold, nudge the Compact Tractor and Irrigation Rig ("You're tilling 39 tiles by hand-ish — the Tractor does 3×3"). I forgot these existed after Day 5.
12. **Add a "Losses" breakdown screen.** An end-of-season summary — "This season you lost 40 crops: 37 out of season, 2 to thirst, 1 to crows" — would've slapped me awake around Day 50 instead of letting me bleed to Day 100.
13. **Give a rescue nudge on season flips.** When the season changes, flag any now-out-of-season crops still in the ground with a "these will wither — harvest or clear them" warning, since the season boundary is exactly when innocent survivors become doomed.
14. **Stretch the goal ladder past the early plateau.** My goals froze at 17/18 on Day 48. Add mid/late goals: "Craft one of every artisan good," "Own one of every building," "Reach Level 15," "Run a fully-automated parcel" — completionist bait that keeps the last 50 days pointed somewhere.
15. **Rebalance or signpost the final land goals.** "Own the whole valley" (all 9 parcels, up to $60k each) is unreachable in a 100-day Classic run; either add interim land goals (own 6, own 7) or make the payoff/telegraphing acknowledge it's a multi-year grind.
16. **Add a crop/recipe/animal Compendium** with a completion tracker ("14/18 crops harvested, 11/14 recipes crafted, 6/6 animals owned"). Give the "touch everything" player an actual scoreboard for touching everything.
17. **Let me pre-plan a season.** A "next season's crops" preview so I can queue what to plant when the calendar flips, instead of guessing and killing a row to find out.

## 6. Systems & Content Analysis

**Worth it, and shining:** The processing chain is the star. Bakery/Creamery/Press/Loom transform a plant-and-harvest loop into a supply-chain game, and the wool→cloth→quilt ladder ($1,500 top end) gives the economy a real ceiling to climb toward. Fertilizer is a clean, always-correct investment (I used it 214 times without a second thought). The drone + sprinkler automation genuinely delivers on the "self-running farm" promise and is a satisfying thing to build toward.

**Underexplained, and it cost me:** *Seasons.* The single most important survival mechanic in the game — the one responsible for 85% of my deaths — is communicated by one rate-limited, two-second toast. The *Greenhouse* is underexplained twice over: its coverage is invisible and its "you need several" nature isn't enforced by any feedback until your crops are already dead. The *Feed Mill* is so quietly presented that a completionist skipped it entirely and paid the cash tax 166 times. These three — season death, greenhouse coverage, feed mill value — are the discoverability black holes.

**Underpowered / underused:** My tool tiers. I never left Rototiller/Hose Cart because the game stopped selling me on the Tractor and Irrigation Rig once I was past the early game. They may be great; I'll never know, and that's a UX miss. Extra craft lanes fall in the same bucket — I never bought one because I never understood the throughput I was leaving on the table.

**What a completionist runs out of:** *New buildings* (frozen at 11 on Day 48), *new goals* (17/18 by Day 48, the 18th unreachable), and *affordable land* (stuck at 5/9). After the halfway mark, "touch everything" had touched everything the game surfaced, and the back 50 days became a cash plateau with no fresh checkbox to chase. The content is rich — 18 crops, 12 buildings, 14 recipes, 6 animals, 9 parcels — but the *guidance and pacing* run dry well before the days do. Give me a compendium, a longer goal ladder, and honest telegraphing of the mechanics quietly killing me, and this becomes a completionist's dream instead of a completionist's very well-documented disaster.
